Projects

Here is the title page of a side-scroller game Samiksha and I made:

And here is a picture of my group's robot during our robotics unit...

Well, the most complex circuit we could make that actually worked was when we connected three jumper wires with two LEDs and a resistor to a button. When we turned on the circuit and pressed the button, both the LEDs turned on, but they were very dim. Basically we connected one jumper wire to Ground and lined it up with the button. The other end of the button connected with the another jumper wire which connected with the two LEDs and resistor. Then the last jumper wire connected with the resistor to the voltage.

After today, my idea of robots has become more complex and broad. It's good to know that, in the future, we can use artificial intelligence to help many people, whether for warfare, medical reasons, or convenience. However, if more of what we rely on becomes even more technological-based in the future, there's the issue of accessibility; anyone could end up using a robot for their own immoral reason rather than the intended reason for which the robot was made.

Overall, being able to work with a group on our robot made the process a lot easier. We were able to figure out things faster (turning on the LEDs) and start experimenting with other parts of the robot. Next time it would be better if we could make our light brighter or even change color.
